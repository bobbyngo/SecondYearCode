~1.0 Exceptions~

Knowing how to handle exceptions is exceptionally important for writing robust and stable applications. Large systems are often riddled with areas where the state, control flow, or the results of an operation are uncertain. It is important to anticipate these problem areas and handle them accordingly. This section details some fundamentals on Exceptions in Java, and how to handle them.

~Task 1.4~

This task will introduce two important new keywords in Java: throw and throws.

Sometimes we want to "throw" an exception on our own. This is often a custom exception that we have implemented. To do this, the throw keyword is used as demonstrated in Main.java.

throws serves a slightly different purpose. The throws keyword is used to denote a method that may throw the specified exception. Specifying exceptions with throws tells the compiler that this method must be handled. Meaning, you will not be able to call this method unless you handle the associated exception.
